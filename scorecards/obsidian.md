# Obsidian

**Category:** Productivity · **Platforms:** iOS, Android, Desktop · **Reviewed:** April 2026

Obsidian is a local-first markdown knowledge base. Notes are plain `.md`
files on your disk; the app adds linking, graph view, and a plugin
ecosystem on top. Paid add-ons (Sync, Publish) are optional and
transparently priced.

## What it does well

- **Local-first.** Your notes are plain files you own. No lock-in; any
  markdown editor can open them.
- **Plugin ecosystem is deep.** ~2000 community plugins for almost any
  specific workflow: calendars, kanban, spaced repetition, dataview queries.
- **Privacy is structural**, not policy. The app has no servers; you
  opt in to Sync if you want cloud sync.
- **Pricing is honest.** Free for personal use forever. Paid add-ons
  are optional and priced clearly.

## Where it falls short

- **Learning curve is steep.** Out of the box it's a markdown editor;
  the value comes from plugins, which you have to discover.
- **Mobile app editing is functional but not delightful**; large
  vaults on iOS/Android can feel slow.
- **Graph view is more eye candy than utility** for most users.
- **Collaboration is essentially absent**; this is a single-user tool.

## Score rationale

- **Accuracy (5/5):** Local markdown files. Nothing to be inaccurate
  about; you own the data.
- **Core utility (4/5):** Strong for personal knowledge management;
  weak for team collaboration (intentionally).
- **Privacy (5/5):** Local-first, audited sync encryption, no ad
  network. Reference-quality.
- **Pricing transparency (5/5):** Fully transparent. Best in this
  scorecard set.
- **UX (3/5):** Functional and customizable; out-of-box polish is
  middling. Power users love it; casual users may not.
