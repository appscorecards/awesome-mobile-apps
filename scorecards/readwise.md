# Readwise

**Category:** Reading / Audio · **Platforms:** iOS, Android, Web · **Reviewed:** April 2026

Readwise syncs highlights from Kindle, Apple Books, Instapaper, PDFs,
Twitter, and other sources into a single review system. A daily review
surfaces a small rotating set of highlights — the spaced-repetition angle
for passive reading.

## What it does well

- **Highlight ingestion is the product.** Kindle sync is reliable,
  Instapaper/Pocket/PDF annotations flow cleanly.
- **Daily review email** is a genuinely good habit — 5-10 highlights
  per day with a light spaced-repetition layer.
- **Export to everywhere.** Notion, Obsidian, Roam, plain markdown,
  RSS. No lock-in.
- **Reader (the companion app)** is now solid for web article reading
  + annotation, effectively replacing Instapaper for many users.

## Where it falls short

- **Price is high** for what is essentially a highlight-aggregator
  plus review. $71.88/year is hard to justify for casual readers who
  highlight a book or two a year.
- **Kindle sync requires browser extension or Amazon credentials**,
  which gives some users pause.
- **Mobile-first reading experience** in Reader still has small
  friction points vs. native Kindle.

## Score rationale

- **Accuracy (4/5):** Sync is reliable; occasional missed highlights
  on obscure sources.
- **Core utility (5/5):** Best-in-class at highlight aggregation and
  review. Nothing else really competes.
- **Privacy (4/5):** Clear policy, no ad network. Kindle credential
  handling is a sticking point.
- **Pricing transparency (4/5):** Price shown clearly; annual vs.
  monthly slightly hidden.
- **UX (4/5):** Clean; Reader is improving steadily.
