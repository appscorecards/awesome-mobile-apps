# Strava

**Category:** Fitness · **Platforms:** iOS, Android, Web · **Reviewed:** April 2026

Strava is the de facto social run/ride tracker. GPS-based activity logging
plus a layer of kudos, comments, and segment competitions. Integrations
with most GPS watches and bike computers are plug-and-play.

## What it does well

- **Social layer is genuinely useful.** Segment leaderboards, local route
  discovery, and kudos keep users consistent. The retention math here is
  real.
- **Third-party integrations are broad.** Garmin, Wahoo, Coros, Polar,
  Apple Watch, Zwift — data flows in cleanly from most sources.
- **Route discovery** and "popular segments" surface local trails and
  climbs that other apps don't.

## Where it falls short

- **Subscription paywall has crept.** Features that used to be free
  (monthly fitness, beacon, some segments) now require paid. Users who
  started pre-2023 feel the erosion.
- **Privacy defaults are permissive.** Default activity visibility is
  "Everyone"; the privacy zones feature exists but isn't prominent.
- **Pricing transparency is mixed.** Annual vs. monthly pricing isn't
  always shown up front; regional pricing variations surprise users.

## Score rationale

- **Accuracy (4/5):** Depends on the underlying GPS device; Strava's
  own phone GPS is acceptable but watches are better.
- **Core utility (4/5):** Social + activity logging combo is best in
  category.
- **Privacy (3/5):** Permissive defaults. Users need to configure
  privacy zones manually.
- **Pricing transparency (3/5):** Subscription features have shifted
  over time without clear upfront disclosure.
- **UX (4/5):** Mobile app is polished; web feed has some clutter.
