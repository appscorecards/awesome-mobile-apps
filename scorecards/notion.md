# Notion

**Category:** Productivity · **Platforms:** iOS, Android, Web, Desktop · **Reviewed:** April 2026

Notion is a document-and-database hybrid widely used for wikis, project
management, and personal knowledge bases. Its core strength is flexibility;
its core weakness is that flexibility can become over-engineering.

## What it does well

- **Block-based editor** is consistent across docs, tables, databases,
  and embedded views. Everything composes.
- **Template ecosystem** is deep. For common use cases (CRM, meeting
  notes, content calendar) there's usually a good starting template.
- **Team collaboration** works well at 5-50 person scale.
- **Web clipper and imports** from Evernote, Confluence, etc., are solid.

## Where it falls short

- **Mobile app has real latency** on large workspaces. Page loads stall.
- **AI features are subscription add-ons** that push total cost up
  noticeably for teams.
- **Local-first users are not the target.** No offline-primary mode;
  everything is cloud-first.
- **Search is serviceable but not excellent**; workspaces past ~500
  pages start to show it.

## Score rationale

- **Accuracy (4/5):** Data handling and sync are reliable.
- **Core utility (4/5):** Strong for small teams and personal use;
  degrades at larger team sizes.
- **Privacy (3/5):** Cloud-first, AI features process content on
  external infrastructure. Policy is clear but model is not minimal-data.
- **Pricing transparency (4/5):** Clear tier pricing, clear feature
  gates.
- **UX (4/5):** Polished desktop; mobile is the weak link.
