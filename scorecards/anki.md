# Anki

**Category:** Learning · **Platforms:** iOS, Android, Desktop, Web · **Reviewed:** April 2026

Spaced-repetition flashcard system with an open-source core. Used broadly
for language learning, medical school, and technical study. The underlying
SRS algorithm and deck format are the reference implementation most
other SRS tools base themselves on.

## What it does well

- **Spaced-repetition is the point**, and Anki does it well. Reviews are
  scheduled by an SRS algorithm (the FSRS variant in recent versions) that
  is well-studied and tunable.
- **Plugin ecosystem** on the desktop client is deep. Users build their
  own card types, import tools, and automation.
- **Shared deck library** (AnkiWeb) hosts millions of community-built
  decks across every topic.
- **Local-first.** Cards are yours; no subscription required on Android or
  desktop; iOS one-time fee of $29.99 funds core development.

## Where it falls short

- **UX is rough** by 2026 standards. The app looks like the 2012-era
  desktop app, because it is the 2012-era desktop app. Users familiar with
  modern app polish find it jarring.
- **Initial card-authoring is tedious.** Good decks take real time to build;
  most users rely on shared decks, which vary in quality.
- **iOS price is a one-time $29.99** which is honest funding but a
  barrier for casual iOS users. Android and desktop are free.

## Score rationale

- **Accuracy (5/5)**: SRS scheduling is mathematically sound and has
  decades of empirical support.
- **Core utility (5/5)**: Unmatched for serious spaced-repetition use.
- **Privacy (5/5)**: Local-first, no tracking, no ad network.
- **Pricing transparency (4/5)**: Clear but the iOS fee surprises some
  users after they start on free Android/desktop.
- **UX (3/5)**: Functional, not pretty. If you care about polish,
  alternatives (RemNote, Obsidian Spaced Repetition) may suit better.
