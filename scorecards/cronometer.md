# Cronometer

**Category:** Nutrition · **Platforms:** iOS, Android, Web · **Reviewed:** April 2026

Cronometer is a long-running manual-entry nutrition tracker focused on
comprehensive micronutrient tracking. Draws primarily from the USDA
National Nutrient Database and other vetted sources rather than user
submissions.

## What it does well

- **Nutrient coverage is the deepest** of any consumer nutrition app we
  reviewed. 84+ nutrients including individual amino acids, fatty-acid
  breakdowns, and less-common minerals. Closer to research-tool depth than
  consumer-app depth.
- **Database quality is high** because entries come from vetted sources,
  not a user-contribution system. Fewer obvious errors than large
  crowd-sourced databases.
- **Free tier is genuinely useful**, not a demo. Core logging and nutrient
  tracking work without a subscription. Gold unlocks some reports and a
  handful of convenience features.
- **Web app** exists and is a peer to the mobile app. Useful for users
  who prefer desktop data entry.

## Where it falls short

- **Logging is slow.** Manual search is the primary workflow; barcode
  scanning exists but isn't as fluid as in newer apps. For a full day of
  meals, cumulative logging time adds up.
- **UX feels dated** compared to 2024–2026 apps. Navigation is functional
  but not elegant. Information density is high, which is both a pro and
  a con depending on user skill level.
- **No photo logging.** Users who want to snap a photo and have the app
  handle the rest won't find that here.

## Score rationale

- **Accuracy (4/5)**: Entries are vetted and generally reliable. Not a
  5 because manual-entry workflows inherently introduce portion-estimation
  error that depends on the user's kitchen scale habits.
- **Core utility (5/5)**: Best-in-category for nutrient depth. Nothing
  else tracks this many nutrients with a free tier.
- **Privacy (4/5)**: Reasonable policies, no ad network. Business model
  is subscription-first.
- **Pricing transparency (4/5)**: Clear pricing page, clear tier
  differences.
- **UX (3/5)**: Functional but dated. Learning curve is real, especially
  for the custom-food editor.
