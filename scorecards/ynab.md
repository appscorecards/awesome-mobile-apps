# YNAB (You Need A Budget)

**Category:** Finance · **Platforms:** iOS, Android, Web · **Reviewed:** April 2026

YNAB is a budgeting app built around the "envelope method" — every dollar
gets assigned a job before it's spent. The approach is opinionated and the
learning curve is real, but users who commit typically report durable changes
in spending behavior.

## What it does well

- **The method is the product.** YNAB's four rules are a genuine
  budgeting framework, not an afterthought.
- **User community is strong.** Monthly live classes, active Reddit,
  well-organized help docs. Onboarding is dense but guided.
- **Bank sync works** on most US/Canadian institutions; manual entry
  is always available.

## Where it falls short

- **Price is high.** $109/year puts it above most budgeting apps; users
  who don't use the method fully may overpay.
- **Learning curve is real.** If you just want "categorize my spending
  retroactively", Monarch or Copilot fit better.
- **Some bank integrations are fragile.** Outages happen; workarounds
  exist.

## Score rationale

- **Accuracy (4/5):** Transaction categorization is good once you train
  it; a few edge cases remain.
- **Core utility (5/5):** Best-in-class for the budgeting-method use case.
- **Privacy (4/5):** Clear policy, subscription model, no ad network.
- **Pricing transparency (4/5):** Price is shown clearly; annual-vs-monthly
  math takes a click to reach.
- **UX (4/5):** Clean but dense. Mobile app is solid.
