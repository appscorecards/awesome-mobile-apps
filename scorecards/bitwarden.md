# Bitwarden

**Category:** Privacy · **Platforms:** iOS, Android, Web, Desktop, CLI · **Reviewed:** April 2026

Open-source password manager with client apps across every major platform.
Generally regarded as the leading free alternative to 1Password and LastPass.

## What it does well

- **Open source** — clients and server implementations are publicly auditable
  and have been reviewed by independent security firms.
- **Cross-platform fidelity** is excellent. The iOS, Android, browser
  extension, desktop app, and CLI all stay in sync.
- **Free tier is genuinely usable**, not crippled. Unlimited passwords,
  multiple devices, and TOTP on the paid tier for $10/year — which is
  the cheapest serious password manager on the market.
- **Self-hosted option** exists (Vaultwarden is a third-party server
  implementation that speaks the same API).

## Where it falls short

- **UX is utilitarian**, not delightful. Autofill flows on iOS are
  occasionally fiddly, and the desktop app's UI has rough edges.
- **Customer support on the free tier is slow.** Community forums are
  the fastest path to answers for non-paying users.
- **Learning curve for the CLI and self-hosted path** is real. Most users
  won't need these features, but power users who try can lose an afternoon.

## Score rationale

- **Accuracy (5/5)**: Not applicable in the same sense as a nutrition app;
  scored on reliability — zero data-loss incidents in years of general use.
- **Core utility (5/5)**: Does the core job across platforms.
- **Privacy (5/5)**: Open source, audited, clear architecture.
- **Pricing transparency (5/5)**: Free tier, $10/year premium, $40/year
  family — stated clearly up front.
- **UX (3/5)**: Utilitarian. 1Password still wins on polish for users
  who are willing to pay for it.
