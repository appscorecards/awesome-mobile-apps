# MacroFactor

**Category:** Nutrition · **Platforms:** iOS, Android · **Reviewed:** April 2026

MacroFactor is a calorie- and macro-tracking app built around an adaptive
algorithm that updates the user's maintenance calorie estimate every week
from scale readings and logged intake. Built by the team behind the
Stronger By Science podcast and Data Driven Strength blog.

## What it does well

- **Adaptive-target algorithm** is the differentiator. Instead of picking
  a static "2000 kcal/day" goal from a questionnaire, the app back-solves
  the user's maintenance from their actual weekly intake and scale trend.
  Over 3–4 weeks this produces a noticeably more stable target than
  static-goal apps.
- **Food database entries are curated**, not crowd-sourced. Fewer entries
  overall than MyFitnessPal but much higher average accuracy per entry.
- **Pricing is transparent.** The subscription model, free trial, and
  family plan are shown clearly up front.

## Where it falls short

- **No photo logging.** Logging is manual search, barcode scan, or meal
  re-logs. For users whose drop-off point is manual entry friction, this
  app won't solve that problem.
- **Learning curve is steeper** than beginner apps. The app surfaces
  concepts like "trend weight" and "expenditure back-calc" that reward
  users who already track seriously.
- **iOS-first feel.** The Android app is fine but lagged behind iOS on
  feature parity for most of 2024–2025.

## Score rationale

- **Accuracy (5/5)**: Best-in-class macro target accuracy among apps
  we've evaluated, because of the adaptive algorithm plus curated DB.
- **Core utility (4/5)**: Does its job well, but only if you want the
  adaptive-macro approach specifically. Doesn't try to be a full-panel
  micronutrient tool.
- **Privacy (4/5)**: Clear privacy policy, no ad networks.
- **Pricing transparency (5/5)**: Cleanest pricing page among apps we
  reviewed in this category.
- **UX (4/5)**: Clean and information-dense; a beginner might find it
  slightly overwhelming.
